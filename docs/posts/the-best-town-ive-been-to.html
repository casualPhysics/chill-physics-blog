The Best Town I've Been To.

10 April 2026 · Hay-on-Wye

I went to Hay-on-Wye during the Easter weekend. It’s a beautiful town in Wales that was declared an ‘Independent Kingdom and Book Town’ by the bookseller Richard Booth.

Set within the countryside, its main attraction are its independent booksellers.

My personal favourite is converted cinema bookshop. It’s a babel-like temple of second hand books of any niche topic you can find. I especially enjoyed the polar expeditions section, the philosophy section, and the history of evolution section. Books were one pound to four pounds each, which is incredible value for knowledge. And all of this set within this stunning backdrop.

1. Gleick, J., Chaos

4. Russell, C. A., Science and Social Change, 1700–1900

5. Keynes, J. M., A Tract on Monetary Reform

6. Le Guin, U. K., The Dispossessed

7. Lenin, V. I., Selected Works, Volume 3

8. Locke, J., An Essay Concerning Human Understanding

9. Wells, H. G., The War of the Worlds

10. Mountevans, Admiral Lord, The Desolate Antarctic

11. Rousseau, J.-J., The Discourses and Other Early Political Writings

12. Tolansky, S., Introduction to Atomic Physics
